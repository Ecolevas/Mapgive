Why Map?

Anytime disaster strikes, there is a scramble for data to get help to the scene. First responders and humanitarian coordinators need maps. But not just any maps - they need access to digital geographic data that can be extracted and shared. The goal is to have maps ready before a crisis occurs.

What Can I Do?

No special skills are needed to join the global community of volunteer mappers. You can be boots on the ground or a million miles away. Volunteers get access to U.S. government purchased, unclassified satellite imagery in a format that is easy to consume with mapping software most anyone can learn.

Many sites offer points of entry for people interested in getting involved in humanitarian crowd-sourced mapping initiatives. This site provides one path. Start Mapping
